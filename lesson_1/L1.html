What we cover

  • Why “progress” means different things in software 1.0 vs. ML vs. gen-AI — one word, many meanings.
  • An eval as an experiment that estimates a quantity, not a deterministic check.
  • Metrics, test sets, and the sealed test set; the ML and gen-AI classifier as worked examples.
  • Why a single number is an estimate, and how sample size shapes how much to trust it.
  • Class imbalance — why a “95% accurate” classifier might just be predicting the majority class.
  • A map of the recurring sources of uncertainty we’ll meet across the course.

Understand · report · reduce — sampling noise

  • Understand — a score on a test set is one draw; sample size drives how much it wobbles between runs.
  • Report — quote the sample size, and give the score as a plausible range, not a bare point.
  • Reduce — use a larger, fairer test set; re-run before believing a change.

Skills you unlock

  • Understand that an eval is now an estimation of an unknown quantity, not a pass/fail check.
  • Estimate the noise around a result and know how to report it.
  • Understand why the “test case” approach can be a trap — and how to avoid it.
