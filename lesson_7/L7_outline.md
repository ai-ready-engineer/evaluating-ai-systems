# Lesson 7 — Additional sources of errors and uncertainty — and why they compound

**Number:** L7
**Tagline:** An eval isn't run once — it has to keep meaning something as the system and the world move.
**Source of uncertainty:** Drift & compounding

## What we cover

- Drift and model evolution — yesterday's eval doesn't carry forward
- Uncertainties **compound** — every source from L1–L6 stacks, and the real uncertainty in a number is their combination
- Re-baselining: when to redo the eval, and how to compare results across model versions
- Design → run → report an eval honestly; the eval-driven improvement loop

## Skills you unlock

- Re-baseline an eval on a schedule — and after every model or world change
- Estimate the *combined* uncertainty of an eval — not just the largest single source
- Report design, method, and caveats so a result can be challenged honestly
- Design an eval that survives the next quarter, not just the next release

## Uncertainty dimension discussed

**Drift, model evolution, and the compounding of every uncertainty source from L1–L6.** The eval ages as the world moves, and the sources stack: the real uncertainty in a number is their *combination*, not the largest single one.
