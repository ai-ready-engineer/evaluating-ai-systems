What we cover

  • Programmatic checks for the “easy” cases — exact match, format and schema checks, JSON validation.
  • When there is no clean ground truth — reference-based metrics, and LLM-as-judge.
  • Score types: binary (0/1), multiclass, ordinal (1–5), continuous.
  • Bias and noise around a score — the same AI answer can be scored differently on re-run, even when the case has a clear answer.
  • A first “judge of judges” — calibrating a judge against humans, sanity-checking the rubric.
  • What “calibration” means for an ML classifier vs. for an LLM judge — two different procedures sharing one word.

Understand · report · reduce — bias and noise around a score

  • Understand — the judge is an instrument with two defects: noise (random wobble, averages out) and bias (systematic offset, more data can’t fix it).
  • Report — give agreement with trusted labels (e.g. Cohen’s κ) and show the rubric, never just the headline number.
  • Reduce — noise: average repeats, lower temperature, sharpen the rubric. Bias: calibrate against a trusted slice.

Skills you unlock

  • Choose a scoring approach that fits the task — programmatic, reference-based, or LLM-as-judge.
  • Write a clear rubric an LLM-as-judge can apply consistently.
  • Spot when variance is coming from the judge, not from the data.
  • Calibrate a judge against human labels before trusting it.
  • Pick the right score type (binary, multiclass, ordinal, continuous) for the output.
