## Understand · report · reduce

The uncertainty this lesson handles: **representativeness & variability** — one number can mean different things, and a sample may not stand for real traffic.

- **Understand it** — one number is used as a proportion, a probability, or a belief; results vary, and a test set may not represent the population it speaks for.
- **Report it** — say which of the trio you mean, attach a spread, and name the population the number stands for.
- **Reduce it** — sample to match real traffic; gather more cases where the spread is widest.

## Content notes

- Hook: "78%, green" — a number + a status color. What claim is that? Unpack it across the lesson.
- The trio, mapped to eval: **proportion** = a fact about your sample (78 of 100 passed); **probability** = the system's true rate / the chance the next case passes; **belief** = how confident we are the true rate is near 78%. The eval gives a proportion; you want the probability; the calculator gives a belief about it.
