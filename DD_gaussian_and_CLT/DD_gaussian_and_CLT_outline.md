# Deep Dive — The Gaussian & the Central Limit Theorem

**Code:** DD_gaussian_and_CLT
**Tagline:** Where statistics came from, and why the normal distribution keeps showing up.
**Status:** Optional, take-home. Background for the curious — not required to follow the lessons.

## What we cover

- A short history of statistics — astronomers and the law of error, Quetelet, Galton & Pearson, Fisher and experiment design
- The normal distribution demystified — not a law of nature, just what a pile of independent noise looks like once you add it up
- The Central Limit Theorem, felt not proven — why individual outcomes aren't normal but *averages* drift toward normal
- Why averages settle and confidence intervals have the shape they do

## Who it's for

The curious who want the "why" behind the language used in L1–L3. No prerequisites; nothing here is needed to follow the eight lessons.

## Where it connects

- L1–L2 — measurement as experiment, the random variable
- L3 — averages settling, noise around a mean (the CLT, felt not proven)
- Deep Dive · Conditional probability & Bayes — the companion on what a probability *means*
